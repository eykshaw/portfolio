2016 | Adobe Illustrator

This poster, made for Design 16, Graphic Design and Computer Technology, showcases the qualities of the typeface Gill San, created by Eric Gill.

This is the rough draft that was chosen to be further developed into the final poster. Ultimately, I decided that the white, grey, and blue colors as well as the negative space best represented the clean look of the typeface, while the alternating heights of the blue letters helped to convey the playful nature of Gill Sans.

The images below show various early drafts leading up to the final product. Gill Sans, known as the Helvetica of the UK, is a fairly common font and is used for the British railroads and various other British products (for example the Puffin books). Because the typeface is so common, I wanted to create a poster that would show the variety of situations that Gill Sans works well in while also showcasing its unique aspects.
