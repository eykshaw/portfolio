November 2016 | Adobe Illustrator

This project was a group project from Design 1, Introudction to Design. Our design focused on redesigning the Segundo Dining Common's courtyard to raise awareness of water conservation and the California drought. The target audience was primarily UC Davis students living in the Segundo dorms, but the posters could also be implemented at a larger scale. The posters shown were designed by me with feedback from my group members.

The poster shown is the final version that we used for our project. Printed, the poster is about 2 feet tall, a size which is meant to be visible and eyecatching from far away, but not completely readable until you get closer. This is because we wanted to draw people into the exhibition area.

This poster is the second version of the rough draft. The colors were ultimately lessed in intensity because the darker blue is less harsh on the eyes and goes with Segundo's overall color scheme better.

This is the first version of the poster. The general format was kept in the final version, but the colors changed fairly drastically because the white didn't stand out as much from further away. The solid colored background also makes the poster more dynamic and interesting to look at.
